# External Analysis — Brazil Last-Mile Moto Delivery

## PESTLE Snapshot
| Factor | Observations | Impact | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Political | Municipal caps on moto courier speed; city-specific license requirements | Medium | São Paulo draft mobility ordinance (Mar 2025) |
| Economic | Inflation easing but fuel costs volatile; gig earnings under pressure | High | IBGE CPI March readout; fuel index volatility |
| Social | Strong WhatsApp penetration; riders value flexible hours | Medium | Meta Brazil usage survey; driver interviews |
| Technological | Competitors rolling out live-route optimization; WhatsApp Cloud API stable | High | Competitor release notes; Meta changelog |
| Legal | Data protection (LGPD) requires explicit consent for location tracking | Medium | LGPD guidance on telematics |
| Environmental | Emissions zones proposed in central districts; electric moto incentives growing | Low/Medium | SP emissions pilot; EV subsidy news |

## Implications & Watchlist
- Opportunities: Lean into WhatsApp-native CX; pilot electric moto incentives in emissions zones; differentiate on SLA reliability.
- Risks: Driver payout inflation squeezes margin; regulatory shifts on telematics and emissions.
- Data Gaps: Precise competitor payout curves; updated municipal policy timelines.
- Check-in Cadence: Refresh monthly; ad-hoc after regulatory announcements.
